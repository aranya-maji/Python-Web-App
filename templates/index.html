Lung Cancer Detection Using Random Forest Algorithm

Random Forest is an ensemble learning technique that combines multiple decision trees to make predictions. In the context of cancer detection, each decision tree can be seen as an individual "expert" that examines specific features of patient data to make predictions. To detect cancer, a wide range of patient data features may be used, including medical history, genetic information, diagnostic test results, lifestyle factors, and more. Random Forest helps select the most relevant features for making predictions, improving the accuracy and reducing overfitting. Random Forest builds multiple decision trees during the training phase. Each tree uses a subset of the training data and a subset of the features. These trees work independently, making their own predictions based on the provided data. After training the decision trees, the Random Forest combines their individual predictions through a majority vote or weighted averaging. The final prediction is determined by aggregating the outputs of all the decision trees. Random Forest is robust to noisy data, missing values, and outliers, making it a suitable choice for real-world medical datasets where data quality can vary.
